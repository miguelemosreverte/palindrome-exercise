    2) Market Insights

    1. Specialization Premium: Scala commands 2.25x the general software engineer salary in Argentina ($47.8K vs. $21.3K baseline).

    2. Remote Work Multiplier: Remote positions offer 2–3x local market rates; LATAM remote ($30–60K) still undercuts global remote ($110–240K), creating arbitrage opportunity.

    3. Supply Constraint: Only 2.6% of global developers know Scala + 250% LATAM YoY demand growth = seller's market for skilled practitioners.

    4. Vertical Concentration: FinTech and distributed systems roles dominate; skill requirements (Akka, Spark, Kubernetes, functional programming) narrow candidate pool further.

    5. Geographic Anchoring: Despite remote listings, Buenos Aires employers and job posts dominate; indicates local talent concentration and network effects.

    3) Data Quality Assessment

    Strengths

  • Multi-source validation (LinkedIn, Glassdoor, Indeed, GetOnBoard, Jooble, Stack Overflow, TIOBE)

  • Consistent date stamps (March 2026) for current market data

  • Mix of primary listings and aggregated indices

    • Weaknesses

  • Currency inconsistency: Glassdoor ARS figures (161K–181K) lack per-year/month clarity; some appear month-based, others year-based

  • Temporal drift: LinkedIn postings show "1–2 years ago" timestamps mixed with March 2026 data; unclear if active or stale

  • Salary ceiling ambiguity: Intive range (2.76M–3.6M ARS/month) appears outlier; no context on seniority or scope

  • Global/regional conflation: TIOBE and Stack Overflow metrics are worldwide; salary ranges mix Argentina, LATAM, and global benchmarks

  • Limited company profiling: Role distribution by company size/stage not documented

  • Sample representation: 19 Argentina roles adequate for snapshot, but no segment breakdown (startup vs. enterprise hiring ratios)

    • ---

    Recommendation: Use salary ranges with ±20% margin due to currency/unit ambiguity. Prioritize LinkedIn and GetOnBoard posts for recency; cross-validate Glassdoor ARS figures against current exchange rates before citing.
